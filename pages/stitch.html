Stitch

3D printed and prototyped a life-size model of Disney’s Stitch.

Process

3D Model

We used stuffed animals to create curved models of Stitch.

Low Poly Model and Print

The only material we had for construction was cardboard, so we decided to use a low poly version of our model. We 3D printed it in order to fully understand the form of the structure.

Laser Cut Cardboard

We laser-cut 72 pieces of cardboard based on the dimensions of our 3D printed structure. We used paper mache, hot glue, and a lot of duck tape to create the outer structure.

In realizing that animated characters are not built with real world proportions in mind, our primary challenge was engineering an internal structure to hold up Stitch’s large ears.

Final Prototype: A Stitch Piggybank
